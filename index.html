Why Austin?

Austin, home of the Texas Longhorns

Austin is the capital city of the U.S. state of Texas, as well as the seat and largest city of Travis County, with portions extending into Hays and Williamson counties. It has been one of the fastest growing large cities in the United States since 2010. Downtown Austin and Downtown San Antonio are approximately 80 miles (129 km) apart, and both fall along the Interstate 35 corridor. Some observers believe that the two regions may some day form a new "metroplex" similar to Dallas and Fort Worth. Restaurants

My favorite restaurants in Austin

Cypress Grill

A taste of New Orleans in Austin. Serves a wide array of dishes, and has something for children and adults alike. Children have their own menu, and adults may enjoy the daily Happy Hour every day of the week.

Address

4404 W William Cannon Dr Suite L, Austin, TX 78749

What I like about it

Such a wide range of dishes, with different styles of cuisine. Nothing like going full health food for the meal, and then a decadent dessert.

See TripAdvisor

El Arroyo

This restaurant is known for the signs placed out front. There are literally books made up entirely of the different sayings that go on the sign. It honestly needs no other introduction.

Address

1624 W 5th St, Austin, TX 78703

What I like about it

Don't dismiss the signs. They are well worth a trip! The food is also incredible. Fresh, delicious ingredients, and the service is amazing.

See TripAdvisor

Smokin' Oak Wood-Fired Pizza & Taproom

Fresh ingredient wood-fired pizzas with a self-serve tap wall. Near Vandegrift High School, Four Points Middle School, and Concordia University Texas. Less than five minutes from Lake Travis!

Address

8300 N FM 620 Rd Suite K200, Austin, TX 78726

What I like about it

The pizzas are incredible, and I've never come across anything like the self-serve tap wall before while I've travelled. This is great all around.

See TripAdvisor
